Graduation & First Month as a Software Engineer

June 26, 2021, 20:00

Hi, It's been a while :S

So, I graduated with a first class degree in computer science, wowoowow great!

My dissertation went well! I successfully (kinda) made a real-time face mask detection system that can run inference on the edge! using a Raspberry Pi and an NCS2. I made a video demonstration which pretty much goes over everything in a pretty concise format, you can watch it below:



Overall I had a great time at university and it's something I will always look back on, I feel like I and everyone else had a very unique & unconventional time here, not just with COVID, but also the constant back to back years of lecturers going on strike :/, overall it was nice though, met a ton of cool dudes :)

WORK

Okay now to the actual cool(ish) stuff, I got my first dev job!!! The company is called IOTechsys, they specialise in creating state of the art software platform technologies for the internet of things hence the name IOTechsys. They mainly focus on industrial IoT systems. Essentially they (now we) create edge systems to control, manage and monitor IoT devices, ranging from lights and sensors to industrial PLC's and stepper motors. The list of devices and types of devices are pretty vast.

So where do I fit in? I'm a part of the device services team, we deal with the implementation and maintenance of the different protocols of all the IoT devices. Essentially I work on the part of the system that communicates with the devices themselves, as each type of device has it's own way of communicating (via its protocol) my job is to work out how the protocol works and how I can implement/improve it within our system so we can control and manage the devices all within the same edge platform - edgeXpert.

So It's been over a month now since I've been working here, and ... I've learned ALOT. I'll start with the first mistake I made,,,,,,,,,,,

Expectations

Both my expectations and the companies. I initially thought I'd come in and start coding within my first week. I. WAS. WRONG.
To my surprise, my first week at work mainly consisted of learning about the company and familiarizing myself with everyone. I sat through meetings about the company, learned about the tools they used and how they used them and also generally just learning about how to use the products they produce at a client level. Also learning how THEY use Jira and being told off when using it wrong (whoops).

After that, I was given my first tasks - reviewing user documentation of device services that I will be eventually working on. After suggesting some improvements I then was given a task to create a video demonstration of one of the device services, this was again given to me so I could familiarise myself with how edgeXpert works while also providing something useful for the marketing team. You can watch the video I produced HERE.

I say all this because, I initially had the expectation that I'd be coding within the first week, heck even within the first 2/3 weeks, but I was wrong, instead, I was just expected to L E A R N. Learn the process, learn the product, learn how everything operates, and just understand how everything works! whether that was through 1 on 1 meeting, looking at the codebase, or reading up confluence pages, my first 2ish weeks was just about LEARNING. having these initial expectations really did stress me out a TON, but thankfully my manager would constanly remind me that everything is ok and would calm me down ALOT.

Making Mistakes

One thing I hate is being told off, especially when It's for something stupid that I should've already have known. This is also something that would cause me a lot of stress as I always hated the idea of being a burden on people around me or my team. HOWEVER, one thing I quickly learned is that it's ok to NOT be perfect, especially during the learning period, also being told of is NOT a bad thing, the sooner you make a mistake and get told off for doing so, the sooner you won't make it again, and I think that's really important to remember.

Asking For Help

Another thing I really did not do much is ask for help. Throughout uni, I'd just learn to struggle through problems, and usually, It was easy to find a solution. Work is different, especially when the work we are doing is specific to us - aka google can't help lol - so all the knowledge about how to solve a specific problem can either be found by wasting 5hrs of your day or just by simply asking one of the other devs. I was slow at this; initially struggling through stupid problems on my own, slowly I am learning that It is ok to ask for help, even for small things, and MOST OF THE TIME other people enjoy helping out - gives them a break on whatever they are working on.

Comparing yourself to others around you

This one is simple, DON'T DO IT. I was the only recent grad who came in with zero prior work experience trying to compare myself to engineers who have been at the company for over a year that have had engineering jobs in the past. This led me to feel insane amounts of guilt that I wasn't doing enough work compared to them. My manager quickly set me straight and told me to S T O P.

Being Honest

The last thing is just being honest, be honest when you don't understand something, be honest if you are unfamiliar with how things work. Also just being open with how you are currently feeling, thankfully I have a really calm and approachable manager who I can go to at any time, he's understanding about the fact that this is my first job and can easily relate to what im going through which has really helped me out a ton reinsuring me and genreally giving me a huge boost of confidence :)

Closing Thoughts

So to wrap it all up. This is literally my dream job, I work in an amazing city, with really smart amazing people and I'm a software engineer. Things did catch me off guard but I've been slowly working through them, learning and understanding that what I'm going through is what all devs have gone through, so its okay. I'm looking forward to see what's next and can't wait to learn even more than what I already have :)

So uh yeah, this was a long one, if you acc read all this your kinda mad but uh ty?, anyway until next time byuebyebyebyebeyby

Thesis Topic Finalised!!!

October 04, 2020, 11:31

Well, kinda.....

Ok, so it's Sunday morning and it's the end of week3 of the first semester, this means uni is really starting to kick off rn, coursework coming left right and center and of course the final year thesis is still a thing that we all have to do during the mayhem.
Good news is, I've pretty much finalised my topic and it's COVID-19 related which is nice.

Essentially I'm going to create a Mask Detection Model that can run in real time and "on the edge" e.g. bringing computation and data storage all to one local machine, (instead of using cloud computing for example). In my case my mask detection model will run on a Intel Neural Compute Stick 2 connected to either a Raspberry Pi 3 or 4 with a camera module attached. My first goal of the thesis is creating the model and having it run in real time. Then in the case that the accuracy is bad or the network size is too large, or the overall performance of the model is too slow for the compute stick I will try and use various techniques of quantization and pruning to see which one is best suited for the application of mask detection.

Overall im really happy about the project proposal, I't does seem a little ambitious, however, as it's a topic that I actually find interesting im more excited to begin than anything else, I just need to get through the 'boring' part which is this whole literacy review, which I should be doing now instead i'm writing this (:

Flutter Day 1

25th June 2020 09:54

First of all if you are reading this and your name starts with a Y and ends with a N, go away

Ok, Good Morning, I created my first Flutter app and coded in dart for the first time!! I've learned a lot but also slightly overwhelmed by how different it is to code in dart, especially since I've only really done front end coding in HTML/CSS.

So following the tutorial from the Fluter docs I created an app that randomly generates startup company names by taking a random pair of popular words in the English language and displaying them in an infinite list view, This was done using an open source package called english_words. (btw just looking through all the open source packages available on pub.dev is getting me hyped). I extended the app to allow the user to save a word pair in the case they liked it. Link to app on github

So, some things I noted with Flutter, as i've said before it is pretty different from anything else I have used to code with especially it's structure:
Everything is based around what they call 'widgets'. so the text on an app is a widget the padding surrounding the text is a widget, the title bar of the app is a widget and the whole app itself is a widget.
So far from what I can tell there are two different types of widgets; Stateless Widgets - which are immutable so typically used for fixed variables such as titles or colors, and Stateful widgets - which typically expect some sort of change to occur within the application. When creating a Stateful widget however, two classes must be created; first a StatefulWidget class that creates an instance of a State class which is the second class you create. So actually the StatefulWidget class itself is immutable but the State class itself stays alive during the lifetime of that widget! Yea ik kinda confusing but its ok, we move thanks to VScode's flutter extension I can quickly generate boiler plate code to do all this for me :)

Something cool about flutter is how easy it is to test and develop full on native applications that are automatically cross platform, for starters simply plugging in my phone via usb to my computer allows me to test my app on my actual device! pretty cool. Also it's ability to test on cross platform is crazy to me, I can run it natively an IOS emulator, run it on my physical android phone, but also run it as a web app on my browser?? (beta).

Anyway yeh thats what I did yesterday, today im going to carry on with Flutter development and create more apps! Later on I might include code examples and talk through them rather than just being a giant wall of text, I still need to organize this page, lmfao ill do it one day.
